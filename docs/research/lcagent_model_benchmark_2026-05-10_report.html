Executive Take

Default pick: GPT-5.5 with reasoning.effort=low. It gave the best overall balance of answer quality, harness reliability, latency, and cost.
Quality challenger: Claude Opus 4.7 produced the most detailed audit-style review, but GPT-5.5 low still scored higher overall because the default score also includes reliability, cost, cache behavior, and latency. Opus is a verification model, not the economical default.

Kimi K2.6 remains the best budget secondary. Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite is the fastest cheap scout, but shallow. Strict OpenRouter origin routing let Claude Sonnet 4.6 and MiniMax M2.7 complete on retry, but it did not make either a default candidate: Sonnet was slow, costly, and still overclaimed; MiniMax became reliable enough to measure but not accurate enough to route to by default.

Retry runs used OpenRouter provider pins with fallbacks disabled and required parameter support enabled. Temperature was explicit at 0.2 for OpenRouter and direct DeepSeek chat-completions runs; OpenAI Responses and direct Moonshot runs omitted temperature.

A medium-effort follow-up did not change the recommendation. Medium helped pinned Sonnet materially and kept Opus strong, but GPT-5.5 medium became slower, pricier, and less calibrated than GPT-5.5 low.

Interpretation Notes

Why GPT-5.5 low wins

It had a strong final answer, reliable structured-tool behavior, and good cache reuse. The none run was faster, but weaker and more expensive because cache reuse collapsed.

Why Opus Is Special-Case

Claude Opus 4.7 gave a very strong, detailed audit-style answer, but its run cost roughly 4.5x GPT-5.5 low and reported no cached input. It is best treated as a review/verification lane, not the highest overall routing choice.

Why not GPT-5.5 none

It finished quickly, but it inspected more raw lines than GPT-5.5 low, made more doubtful claims, and cost more because cached input dropped from 111,616 to 32,256 tokens.

Why not Gemini, Grok, MiniMax

Gemini's speed is real, but the answers were too shallow or too wrong to displace Kimi. Grok 4.3 completed but inspected very little source and targeted the wrong doc. Strict provider routing fixed MiniMax's completion failure, but not its answer quality.
